08 — FAQ

Fume Hood Testing FAQs

Most standards and safety programs call for annual certification, and many institutions test every 6 to 12 months. Your internal chemical hygiene plan, insurer, or accrediting body may set a specific interval. If you are not sure what applies to your lab, call us and we will help you confirm it.
ASHRAE 110 is the standard method for evaluating fume hood containment using tracer gas. Results are reported as a rating: AM is as manufactured (factory), AI is as installed (in your lab before use), and AU is as used (under normal working conditions). The number after the letter is the tracer gas concentration detected at the breathing zone; lower is better.
We identify the cause on site, whether it is fan speed, a motor issue, sash setup, duct static pressure, or airflow obstruction. Many issues we can correct during the same visit. For larger repairs we give you a clear scope and quote, then retest and certify once the fix is in place.
A standard fume hood test takes roughly 30 to 60 minutes. We schedule around your lab's hours and work hood by hood so the rest of the lab keeps running. For larger sites we stage the work to minimize downtime.
